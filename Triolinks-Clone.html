Frequently Asked Questions

What is Minimum Viable Product (MVP)?
An MVP, or minimum viable product, is a stripped-down version of a potential market product that is designed to test the feasibility of the concept and gauge customer demand. It allows companies to test the waters and gather valuable feedback before fully committing to a product launch, saving time and resources. An MVP typically only includes the core features and highlights the unique selling points of the product, with the intention of expanding and improving upon the MVP in future releases.
Why is MVP important?
An MVP allows startups to solve cash flow issues by releasing a product earlier and without overspending on development. It helps generate revenue from early customers, which can be used for market research, adding desired features, and marketing to a wider audience. MVP development also helps build a user base and establish a market for future products. It is easier to sell to existing customers than to find new ones.
